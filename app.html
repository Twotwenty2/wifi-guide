The Invisible Problem

Why you're lagging on a 1 Gigabit internet plan.

When video buffers or ping spikes, users blame their ISP. In reality, 80% of home connection drops are caused by local environment bottlenecks.

🚗

Channel Overlap

Routers are lazy. When set to "Auto," almost every router in an apartment building selects Channel 6. Your data packets must wait in line behind your neighbors' traffic.

Symptom: Evening Lag
⚡

Radio Interference

Microwaves, cordless DECT phones, baby monitors, and Bluetooth accessories all broadcast on the exact same 2.4 GHz frequency spectrum as your WiFi.

Symptom: Random Drops
🧱

Physical Obstruction

Wood, brick, and metal absorb radio waves. Hiding a router inside a TV media console or behind a kitchen appliance can destroy up to 50% of your signal range.

Symptom: Dead Zones

Golden Rule of WiFi

The 1, 6, 11 Non-Overlapping Mandate

In the 2.4 GHz band, channels 1 through 11 overlap heavily. Selecting intermediate channels like 3, 4, or 8 causes overlapping interference with multiple adjacent networks simultaneously. Change trains users to strictly choose 1, 6, or 11.
